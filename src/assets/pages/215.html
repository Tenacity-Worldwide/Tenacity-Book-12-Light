B9.6 Reading: A poem

This poem is by an Angolan poet, who was also his country’s first president. Read it and
answer the questions below.
Sheets of tin nailed to posts
driven in the ground
make up the house.
Some rags complete
the intimate landscape.
The sun slanting through cracks
welcomes the owner.
After twelve hours of slave
labour
breaking rock
shifting rock
breaking rock
shifting rock
fair weather
wet weather
breaking rock
shifting rock
Old age comes early.
A mat on dark nights
is enough when he dies
gratefully
of hunger.
Agostinho Neto
Translated by Margaret Dickinson
