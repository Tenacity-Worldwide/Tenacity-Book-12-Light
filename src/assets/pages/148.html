B6.7 Language focus: Advantages and disadvantages
There are some special expressions we can use to express advantages and disadvantages:
• (state an advantage), but on the other hand (state a disadvantage).
• Although (state an advantage), (state a disadvantage).
• Despite the fact that (state an advantage), (state a disadvantage).
Note:
In all the examples above, the disadvantage can also be stated before the advantage. Although
and on the other hand are followed by clauses (noun + verb).
• Although globalisation has several disadvantages, it has some advantages too.
• Globalisation has some advantages but on the other hand it also has several disadvantages.
Despite is followed by a noun or noun phrase:
• Despite having some disadvantages, globalisation has some advantages.
• Despite the fact that globalisation has some advantages, it also has several disadvantages.
