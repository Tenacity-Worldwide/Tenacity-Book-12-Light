• In some situations, particularly if two past events occurred close in time or are closely
connected, we can use either the past simple or past perfect. So we can say:
Homo sapiens appeared after language had started to develop. or
Homo sapiens appeared after language started to develop.
• The continuous form of the past perfect emphasises that a past perfect action was continuous
or repeated. For example:
While earlier ancestors of man had been walking around on four legs, hominids had the ability to
walk on two legs.
