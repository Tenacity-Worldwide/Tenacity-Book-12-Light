A5.5 Increase your word power: Initials and acronyms
Initials
• An initial is the first letter of a word.
• Initials are often used as a quick way of referring to names that consist of several words.
Usually we say the initials separately, for example: for the U.S.A. we say you/ess/ay.
Acronyms
 • Some initials can be pronounced as a single word, known as an acronym, for example, AIDS
for Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome. Many well-known acronyms are written in lower
case letters, for example Aids.
