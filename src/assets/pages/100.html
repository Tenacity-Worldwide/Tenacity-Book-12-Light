B4.1 Increase your word power: Film genres
1
Films are usually categorised by genre (type of film). Match the different kinds of film genres

likethis:6=a
a These films include fights, car chases, explosions, natural disasters
and dangerous stunts. The excitement frequently interrupts the plot,
which is carried along by characters who are either good or bad.
b A light-hearted plot with plenty of funny situations that include jokes
or slapstick humour. A happy ending is guaranteed.
c Bank robberies or murders are carried out by realistic criminals who
are hunted down by equally plausible police.
d We are confronted with human situations, often involving difficult
decisions, many of which we can relate to our own lives.
e The aim is to frighten the audience. The settings and characters are
realistic but a monster or ghost is threatening the life of the hero.
f The plot is simple: boy meets girl and their romance is frequently
interrupted by songs and dancing.
g We are given a futuristic vision of life when technology rules, there
are usually lots of special effects.
h The life of a historical person, or an account of a historical event is
served up, often with sumptuous costumes, ceremonies and battle
scenes.
i We are taken to a world which is not ours, and where values are
either much better or worse than our own.
j Boy meets girl; boy loses girl; boy finds girl again and all is well. This
is the usual plot, though sometimes all is not well at the end.
k These films are created by drawings or computer-generated images
rather than real scenes and actors. Plots may be action or comedy.
