Word associations
A common memorisation technique involves associating a new word with something familiar. This
can be another word or several words, or a mental image. The associated word or image should be
vivid or exaggerated to make it memorable. For example:
1 Substitute one word for another. This can be done either in English or Amharic or your first
language.

2 Break up the word and substitute parts of it for other words.
For example: Licence = lie, tell a lie, sense = feel, see, hear, touch
3 Make an acronym.
