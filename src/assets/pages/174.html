B7.6 Language focus: Verb + to infinitive
1 When some verbs are followed by another verb, the second verb is the to infinitive,
for example
I can’t afford to go out.
Let’s agree to differ.
I’ve decided to finish my essay tonight.
I want to go home now.
2 Some verbs are followed by an object before the to infinitive.
I’ve invited all my friends to come to the house.
Our teacher warned us not to give in our homework late.
We must ask someone to help us.
I want my employees to speak foreign languages.
Note: want is a verb that goes in both group 1 and group 2.
