B9.4 Language focus: Highlighting information in a sentence
Look at these questions and answers:
Question: What do you do at weekends?
Answer: What I do at weekends is relax!
Question: How do you prepare for exams?
Answer: How I prepare for exams is first of all I make a revision timetable.
• We can see here that what and how are being used in two different ways.
1 In the questions, they are used as question words.
2 In the answers they are being used as pronouns meaning the thing that … or the things that …
All wh- words can be used in this way.
• When we use wh- words in this way, we are highlighting the information that comes after the
wh- phrase, for example:
Answer: What I do at weekends is relax!
Answer: How I prepare for exams is first of all I make a revision timetable.
